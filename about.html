About

Malcolm James McCormick (January 19, 1992 â€“ September 7, 2018) was an American rapper and record producer better known as Mac Miller. Miller debuted in the Pittsburgh hip hop scene in 2007, at the age of fifteen. He signed a record deal with Rostrum Records in 2010 and released his breakthrough mixtapes K.I.D.S. (2010) and Best Day Ever (2010). (2011). Blue Slide Park (2011), Miller's debut studio album, became the first independently distributed debut album to top the US Billboard 200 since 1995.

He established the record label imprint Remember Music in 2013. He left Rostrum after his second studio album, Watching Movies with the Sound Off (2013), and signed with Warner Bros. Records in 2014. He released GO:OD AM (2015), The Divine Feminine (2016), Swimming (2018), and the posthumous Circles (2020). Swimming was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Rap Album posthumously. Under the alias Larry Fisherman, he also worked as a record producer for various famous artists, including himself.
